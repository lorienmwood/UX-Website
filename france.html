THE 10 BEST THINGS TO DO IN FRANCE

  • DESTINATIONS
  • BLOG
  • ABOUT
  • RESOURCES

    • 1. Visit the Eiffel Tower in Paris

    The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris, stands tall as a testament to the city's beauty and grandeur. This magnificent structure offers visitors an unparalleled experience, with breathtaking views that showcase the splendor of Paris. Whether you choose to take the elevator or climb the stairs, reaching the top of the Eiffel Tower is a journey worth undertaking.

    2. Explore the Louvre Museum

    The Louvre is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. Home to thousands of works of art, including the famous Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo, the Louvre is a cultural treasure trove spanning centuries. Exploring the Louvre is a journey through the history of art, with masterpieces from various periods and civilizations. The museum showcases the artistic achievements of humanity in a grand and awe-inspiring setting.

    3. Wander through the Palace of Versailles

    The Palace of Versailles! Prepare to be captivated by the stunning beauty of this French Baroque architectural masterpiece. Immerse yourself in the opulence of the palace, marvel at the grandeur of its Hall of Mirrors, and stroll through the breathtaking gardens. As the former residence of French kings and queens, the Palace of Versailles holds a rich history and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site. Get ready for an unforgettable experience as you explore the regal splendor and immerse yourself in the grandeur of this historic landmark.

    4. Indulge in French Cuisine

    French cuisine is renowned worldwide. Indulge in classic dishes such as croissants, escargot (snails), coq au vin (chicken in wine sauce), baguettes, and a variety of delightful pastries. To truly savor the authentic gastronomic experience, consider exploring the local markets.

    5. Tour the Mont Saint-Michel

    Mont Saint-Michel is a historic and picturesque site in Normandy, France. It features a medieval abbey on a rocky island commune, offering a glimpse into the region's rich history. Visitors can access the island via a causeway during low tide, adding to the sense of adventure. Inside the abbey, explore its architecture, stained glass windows, and preserved religious artifacts. Mont Saint-Michel also offers charming streets with shops, cafes, and restaurants. Enjoy local cuisine and souvenirs while strolling along the narrow pathways. The breathtaking views of the surrounding bay are a highlight. Whether from the top of the abbey or along the promenade, the panoramic vistas showcase the natural beauty of the area. Overall, Mont Saint-Michel is an enchanting and captivating historic site that takes you on a journey back in time.

    6. Stroll the lavender fields in Provence

    Provence, a region located in the southeastern part of France, is widely celebrated for its breathtaking and picturesque lavender fields. These enchanting landscapes, which are especially captivating during the summer season, captivate visitors with their vibrant and dazzling purple hues. As visitors explore the region, they are greeted with an array of charming and quaint old villages that serve as a captivating backdrop to the lavender fields. This combination of natural beauty and cultural charm creates an unforgettable experience that indulges both the senses and the soul.

    7. Ski in the French Alps

    The French Alps are renowned for providing exceptional opportunities for skiing and snowboarding during the winter season. With its vast expanse of picturesque mountains, Chamonix and Courchevel have emerged as highly sought-after destinations among winter sports enthusiasts. These regions are known not only for their thrilling and challenging slopes but also for their awe-inspiring mountain vistas that take your breath away. Additionally, the quaint and charming alpine villages that dot the landscape add to the overall allure of these locations, creating a truly unforgettable experience for visitors.

    8. Visit the D-Day Landing Beaches in Normandy

    Normandy's D-Day Landing Beaches were crucial in World War II, forever changing history. Omaha Beach is awe-inspiring, with a profound sense of admiration for the soldiers who fought bravely. Normandy American Cemetery is a solemn place where fallen heroes rest. Exploring these beaches gives a deep appreciation for the sacrifices of World War II. It is a humbling reminder of human spirit in adversity.

    9. Discover the Châteaux of the Loire Valley

    The Loire Valley is a picturesque region in France that is renowned for its stunning châteaux (castles) and beautiful gardens. When visiting the Loire Valley, make sure to explore some of its most famous châteaux, such as the grand Château de Chambord and the elegant Château de Chenonceau. These magnificent examples of Renaissance architecture will leave you in awe with their opulence and architectural marvels. Additionally, don't miss the opportunity to discover other hidden gems in the Loire Valley, where you can immerse yourself in the rich history and charm of this enchanting region.

    10. Relax in the French Riviera

    The French Riviera, or Côte d'Azur, is famous for its stunning beaches, delightful coastal towns, and exciting nightlife. Nice, Cannes, and Saint-Tropez provide a perfect blend of sunbathing, water sports, and a vibrant atmosphere that captivates tourists from all over the globe.
