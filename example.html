THE 10 BEST THINGS TO DO IN FRANCE

  • DESTINATIONS
  • BLOG
  • ABOUT
  • RESOURCES

    • 1. Visit the Eiffel Tower in Paris

    The Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of Paris and offers breathtaking views of the city. Visitors can take an elevator or climb the stairs to the top for panoramic scenes of Parisian landmarks such as the Louvre, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Seine River.

    2. Explore the Louvre Museum

    The Louvre is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. Home to thousands of works of art, including the famous Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo, the Louvre is a cultural treasure trove spanning centuries.

    3. Wander through the Palace of Versailles

    The Palace of Versailles is a masterpiece of French Baroque architecture. Explore the opulent palace, its Hall of Mirrors, and the stunning gardens. It was the residence of French kings and queens and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

    4. Indulge in French Cuisine

    French cuisine is celebrated worldwide. Enjoy classic dishes such as croissants, escargot (snails), coq au vin (chicken in wine sauce), baguettes, and an array of delectable pastries. Try local markets for an authentic gastronomic experience.

    5. Tour the Mont Saint-Michel

    Mont Saint-Michel is a medieval abbey perched on a rocky island commune. Accessible by a causeway during low tide, it's a marvel of medieval architecture. Explore the abbey, charming streets, and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding bay.

    6. Stroll the lavender fields in Provence

    Provence is known for its picturesque lavender fields, especially in the summer. The vibrant purple landscapes, often against the backdrop of old villages, create a visually stunning and fragrant experience.

    7. Ski in the French Alps

    The French Alps offer world-class skiing and snowboarding in the winter. Chamonix and Courchevel are popular destinations with challenging slopes, breathtaking mountain scenery, and charming alpine villages.

    8. Visit the D-Day Landing Beaches in Normandy

    Normandy's D-Day Landing Beaches played a pivotal role in World War II. Explore historic sites like Omaha Beach and the Normandy American Cemetery to pay tribute to the Allied forces who landed there on June 6, 1944.

    9. Discover the Châteaux of the Loire Valley

    The Loire Valley is dotted with stunning châteaux (castles) and gardens. Visit Château de Chambord, Château de Chenonceau, and others to experience the opulence and architectural marvels of the Renaissance.

    10. Relax in the French Riviera

    The French Riviera, or Côte d'Azur, is known for its glamorous beaches and charming coastal towns. Nice, Cannes, and Saint-Tropez offer a mix of sunbathing, water sports, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
