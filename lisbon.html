    • THE 5 BEST THINGS TO DO IN LISBON

    1. Discover the charming Alfama District

    Take a delightful walk through the quaint and meandering streets of Alfama, Lisbon's oldest neighborhood. Here, you can discover enchanting historic buildings, picturesque squares, and authentic Fado music houses. Don't forget to pay a visit to the São Jorge Castle for awe-inspiring panoramic vistas of the city.

    2. Explore Belém and its Landmarks

    You'll love visiting the charming Belém district, which is home to incredible landmarks that you shouldn't miss. Make sure to include a visit to the stunning Belém Tower and the magnificent Jerónimos Monastery, both of which are recognized as UNESCO World Heritage Sites. And while you're there, don't forget to indulge in the delicious and world-famous Pastel de Nata at Pastéis de Belém, a renowned pastry shop.

    3. Enjoy Tram 28

    Hop on a delightful Lisbon tram ride aboard Tram 28, as it takes you on a journey through some of the city's most charming neighbourhoods, including Graça, Alfama, Baixa, and Estrela. It's an excellent opportunity to admire the city's unique architecture and breathtaking landscapes.

    4. Visit the Jerónimos Monastery

    Belém is a truly amazing historic district with so many iconic landmarks. You definitely don't want to miss Belém Tower, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a true symbol of Portugal's Age of Discovery. And let's not forget about the incredible Jerónimos Monastery nearby, which is an absolute masterpiece of Manueline architecture. Both of these sites beautifully showcase Portugal's rich maritime history and are absolutely must-visit attractions that you won't want to miss out on.

    5. Enjoy the Stunning View from Miradouros

    Indulge yourself in the breathtaking panoramic views of Lisbon from its numerous miradouros (viewpoints). Miradouro da Senhora do Monte, Miradouro da Graça, and Miradouro da São Pedro de Alcântara are highly recommended spots to immerse yourself in the mesmerising vistas of the city and the Tagus River.
